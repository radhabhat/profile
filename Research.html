Research Interest

My specialisation is on cryptography, a key enabling technology for cybersecurity. In cryptography, my primary focus is on developing theoretical and practical privacy-preserving information storage and retrieval techniques. My general research interests lie in the use and development of privacy-preserving information storage and retrieval techniques for effective information processing and access. I am particularly interested in constructing Private Information Retrieval (PIR) and its varients in distributed environments such as privacy-preserving access control and tamper evident Cloud access. My other areas of interest include Oblivious Transfer (OT), oblivious RAM, asymmetric encryption (e.g., homomorphic, non-malleable), open source debugging (e.g., Linux). Now a days, most people find themselves engaging in retrieving information in two different scenarios: i) correct information in almost real-time, and ii) private information from untrusted sources to withstand against data selling. Also, some large group of people involved in protecting their information stored in a remote storage environments such as Cloud and Big Data. I am interested in developing a novel and efficient techniques for the above scenarios.

My secondary interest lies in the area of parallel computer architecture and programming using MPI, OpanCL and CUDA.
